Frankenstein By Mary Shelley

Frankenstein by Mary Shelley is a groundbreaking Gothic novel that explores themes of ambition, creation, and the consequences of defying nature. The story follows Victor Frankenstein, a young scientist who becomes obsessed with unlocking the secrets of life. Driven by his ambition, he creates a sentient creature from dead body parts, only to be horrified by its appearance and abandon it. The creature, longing for acceptance but met with rejection and cruelty, turns vengeful, leading to a tragic cycle of violence and suffering. Through its dual narrative of creator and creation, *Frankenstein* raises profound questions about morality, responsibility, and the dangers of unrestrained scientific exploration. Often regarded as the first science fiction novel, it remains a timeless exploration of human nature and the pursuit of knowledge.
