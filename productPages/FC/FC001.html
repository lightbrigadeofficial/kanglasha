To Kill A Mockingbird By Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee is a powerful novel set in the 1930s in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama. The story is narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl, and follows her coming-of-age journey as her father, Atticus Finch, a principled lawyer, defends Tom Robinson, a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout’s eyes, the novel explores deep themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and the loss of innocence. The book highlights the prejudices and injustices present in society while also showcasing the courage and integrity of those who fight for what is right. It is a poignant commentary on human nature and the struggle for equality.
