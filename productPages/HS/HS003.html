Mossad Exodus By Gad Shimron

Mossad Exodus by Gad Shimron is a gripping account of one of the most daring and secretive operations in Israel's history. The book focuses on the Mossad's involvement in the rescue of Jewish Ethiopian refugees during the 1980s, a mission known as Operation Moses. Shimron, a former Mossad agent, provides a detailed and thrilling narrative of the covert operation that brought thousands of Ethiopian Jews to Israel, often under dangerous and covert conditions. Through firsthand experiences and insights, the book sheds light on the complex and morally challenging decisions made by the Mossad agents, as well as the political and human factors that shaped the operation. It's a compelling exploration of espionage, human rights, and the lengths to which nations will go to safeguard their people.
